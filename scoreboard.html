Scale benchmark — the chase for Muon (10.7M char-GPT on text8)

d384 · 6 layers · ctx 128, stochastic batches, 2000 steps, 100× beyond meta-training. The plateau that stalled v10/v12 at ~2.4–2.5 was diagnosed (not state collapse but under-damped oscillation, then a spectral limit) and fixed: v13 adds momentum + Newton–Schulz orthogonalization — Muon's two mechanisms, in our equivariant framework, everything else learned.

Result: v13 closed 85% of the gap (1.13 → 0.16 nats). λ=0.5 reaches 1.45 and is still descending where Muon (1.29) has flattened. Head-to-head v13 vs tuned Muon: v13 is faster for the first ~300 steps (step 200: 1.86 vs 1.98) — a per-step win at short horizons — then Muon's mid-phase pulls ahead. v13 also beats tuned Muon at 20 steps on covertype and pendigits, and beats it on nanoGPT@100. Open: mid/late-phase rate (finer λ, longer horizon where v13 still descends), and small image-MLP@20 (v14 blend). Wall-clock ~2× Muon, optimization pending.

Milestones

  1. v2MNIST-only distro; matches tuned Muon on 100-step MNIST probe
  2. v4task zoo; first zero-shot wins over ALL tuned baselines (pendigits@20)
  3. v5time inputs + cross-layer + capacity; calibration fixed (lambda*=1)
  4. v7noise + width variation; beats Muon on MNIST h16@20; wins nanoGPT@100
  5. v8width support to 96: fashion-h64 probe 46-68% -> 93%
  6. v9resampled episodes (real SGD), d<=128 nanoGPTs, OpenML-54 population
  7. v10muP fan-in gauge: zero-shot descends at 10.7M, early parity with Muon
  8. v11horizon episodes to 2000: NEGATIVE, destabilized (plateau was not horizon)
  9. v12learned momentum: damps oscillation but plateau holds (it was a symptom)
  10. v13+ spectral orthogonalization: BREAKS plateau, beats Muon per-step <300 steps
  11. v14learned blend rho: orthogonalize big matrices only (recover small MLPs) - TRAINING
